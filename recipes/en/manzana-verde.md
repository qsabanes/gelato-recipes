# Green Apple Sorbet (800g)

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Green apple (Granny Smith)** | **400g** | *Weight peeled and cored.* Aromatic base. |
| **Still water** | **140g** | Solvent for the syrup. |
| **Lemon juice** | **40g** | High dose; slows oxidation and adds acidity. |
| **Sucrose** | **40g** | Low amount; structural support only. |
| **Dextrose** | **125g** | High dose (very high PAC); keeps the sorbet soft at -18 °C with barely any sweetness. |
| **Inulin** | **50g** | Vital: mimics the texture of fat and stops it being a granita. |
| **Fine salt** | **1g** | Brings out the green flavour of the apple. |
| **Guar gum** | **2.5g** | A heaped half teaspoon; sorbets need more stabiliser. |

---

## Procedure: cold assembly

So the apple tastes of green fruit and not of baked apple, we separate the hot part from the fruit.

### Step 1: Neutral syrup (thermal base)

1. Put the **140g of water** in the pan.
2. In a dry bowl, mix all the powders (sucrose, dextrose, inulin, salt and guar).
3. At **50 °C**, add the powders in a steady stream, whisking.
4. Raise to **85 °C** for 1 minute to dissolve the sugars and activate the inulin and guar. You're left with a thick, transparent syrup.
5. Transfer it to a tub and **cool it completely in the fridge** (or a reverse bain-marie with ice). It's essential that it's cold for the next step.

### Step 2: Acid medium

1. Squeeze and clean **40g of lemon juice**.
2. Put it in the blending beaker together with the cold syrup from Step 1.

### Step 3: Quick blending

1. Peel and core the **Granny Smiths** quickly so they don't oxidise (you need 400g net).
2. Drop them straight into the cold liquid (syrup + lemon); the low pH cuts off the enzymatic oxidation.
3. Immersion blender at full power for **2-3 minutes**. The already-active stabiliser traps the fibre and you're left with a silky, whitish-green purée.

### Step 4: Maturation and churning

1. Mature in the fridge for **4-6 hours** (enough for the apple fibre to hydrate).
2. Into the empty machine. With no fat, sorbets freeze quickly: **15-20 minutes**.

> For an even more acidic profile, you can add 2-3 g of malic acid (the natural acid of green apple) to the bowl of powders.
