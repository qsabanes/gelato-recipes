# Watermelon Sorbet (800g)

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Juiced watermelon juice** | **530g** | Total liquid base. No added water. |
| **Sucrose** | **50g** | Base sweetness. |
| **Dextrose** | **90g** | High PAC; stops the watermelon's 92% water from turning into an ice block. |
| **Inulin** | **30g** | High dose of dry solids to give body. |
| **Lemon juice** | **20g** | Boosts the flavour and maintains the pH. |
| **Guar gum** | **0.6g** | 1/4 teaspoon. Traps the fruit's free water. |

---

## Procedure (master syrup)

To avoid heating all the watermelon and spoiling its flavour, we use an interface technique.

1. **Juicing and filtering:** blend the pulp and strain it fine (no seeds or coarse fibre). Set **430g** aside in a jug and put it in the fridge (cold phase).
2. **Master syrup (hot):** put the remaining **100g** of juice in a pan with all the powders (sucrose, dextrose, inulin and guar). Heat to **85 °C** for 1 minute, whisking well. Using only part of the juice hydrates the guar and dissolves the sugars with minimal impact on the flavour.
3. **Thermal union:** pour the hot syrup over the 430g of cold juice. Add the lemon juice and run the immersion blender for 1 minute. You're left with a lukewarm, intensely pink mixture.
4. **Maturation and churning:** mature in the fridge for 6-12 hours. Apply the pre-freeze (15 min in the freezer) and into the machine.

> Variant: 3-4 fresh mint leaves or a pinch of ground pink pepper in the last immersion-blender step.
