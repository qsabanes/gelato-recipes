# Mango Sorbet (800g)

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Mango pulp** | **350g** | Peeled, blended and strained (no strings). |
| **Still water** | **245g** | Solvent for the matrix. |
| **Sucrose** | **78g** | Low dose; ripe mango is already sweet. |
| **Dextrose** | **53g** | High PAC so it's scoopable. |
| **Inulin** | **20g** | Solids that mimic the fatty sensation. |
| **Lemon juice** | **30g** | Regulates the pH; prevents oxidation and highlights the flavour. |
| **Guar gum** | **0.4g** | A pinch; the mango's pectin does the rest. |

> Variant: add 2 basil leaves when blending the mango for an aromatic contrast.

---

## Procedure

1. **Fruit (cold):** peel the mangoes, remove the stone and blend the pulp. **Strain it through fine mesh** to eliminate the long fibres that would ruin the texture. Immediately add the 30g of lemon juice to the pulp to block oxidation.
2. **Syrup (hot):** mix the water with the sucrose, the dextrose, the inulin and the guar. Heat only to **70-80 °C**, blending with the immersion blender to properly hydrate the sugars and the hydrocolloid.
3. **Combining:** let the syrup cool to lukewarm and pour it over the cold mango pulp. Run the immersion blender at full power for 2 minutes.
   - **Important:** do not heat the mango pulp. If you cook it, you lose the fresh compounds and it tastes of jam.
4. **Maturation:** fridge **6-12 hours** so the mango's pectin and the guar bind the free water.
5. **Churning:** 10 seconds of immersion blender to break the gel and into the machine with the motor running. Ready in **15-20 minutes**.
