# Lemon & Basil Sorbet (800g)

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Water** | **408g** | Solvent. |
| **Lemon juice** (fresh) | **180g** | Citric acid and aroma (pH ≈ 2.4). |
| **Sucrose** | **100g** | Structure and sweetness. |
| **Dextrose** | **80g** | PAC so it's scoopable. |
| **Inulin** | **30g** | Solids (body) and a fatty sensation without fat. |
| **Guar gum** | **0.8g** | 1/3 teaspoon. Viscosity. |
| **Fresh basil** | **15-20 leaves** | Aromatic terpenes (linalool). |
| **Lemon zest** | **2 lemons** | Essential oils (limonene). |

---

## Procedure (anti-oxidation)

### 1. Base syrup

Mix the **water** with the sucrose, the dextrose and the inulin. Heat to **70 °C**.

- **Hydration:** add the guar and run the immersion blender for 1 minute. The syrup should turn slightly thick.

### 2. Blanching the basil

If you blend the basil cold, polyphenol oxidase turns it brown within minutes.

- **Thermal shock:** drop the leaves into the hot syrup (70 °C) for **30 seconds**.
- **Colour fixing:** remove them and put them into a beaker with the **cold lemon juice**. The acid and the cold halt the degradation of the chlorophyll.

### 3. Terpene extraction

Pour the hot syrup into the beaker of juice with the blanched leaves.

- **Homogenisation:** immersion blender at full power for 2 minutes until no leaf pieces remain.
- **Filtration:** strain through fine mesh or cheesecloth to remove the fibre. You're left with a bright green syrup.

### 4. Maturation

Let it cool and store in the fridge for **6-12 hours**. Maturation fully hydrates the guar and the inulin and stabilises the essential oils in solution.

### 5. Churning

Run the immersion blender for 10 seconds before churning. Into the machine, **15-20 minutes** (sorbets set faster than cream gelatos).
