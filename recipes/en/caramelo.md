# Caramel Gelato (800g)

Two phases: the caramel sauce (the swirl) and the gelato base.

---

## Phase 1: Caramel sauce (swirl)

*Make it a few hours ahead or the day before so it's properly cold.*

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Sucrose** (white sugar) | **100g** | The body of the caramel. |
| **Cream (35% fat)** | **100g** | Fat so the caramel doesn't set into a hard crystal. |
| **Dextrose** | **30g** | Antifreeze so the pocket doesn't freeze. |
| **Butter** | **20g** | Adds richness and lipids. |
| **Fine salt** | **2-3g** | Flavour contrast and an extra drop in PAC. |

**Process:**

1. **Dry caramel:** put the sugar over medium heat without touching it. Wait for it to melt and reach a dark amber (pyrolysis). Go too far and it tastes burnt; stop too early and it just tastes of sugar.
2. **Deglazing:** heat the cream separately. Once the sugar is caramel, add the cream carefully (it releases steam, it's an exothermic reaction) and stir without stopping.
3. **Finishing:** add the dextrose, the butter and the salt. Run the immersion blender for a perfect emulsion.
4. **Cooling:** store it in a squeeze bottle or jar in the fridge. It must be thoroughly cold before it touches the gelato.

---

## Phase 2: Caramel gelato base (800g)

We caramelise part of the base's sugar so the whole gelato carries the flavour, not just the pockets.

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Whole milk** | **450g** | Continuous phase. |
| **Cream (35% fat)** | **130g** | Dairy fat. |
| **Milk powder** | **35g** | MSNF (proteins). |
| **Sucrose** (to caramelise) | **50g** | Base caramel flavour. |
| **Sucrose** (plain) | **30g** | Sweetness. |
| **Dextrose** | **55g** | PAC. |
| **Inulin** | **15g** | Solids and texture. |
| **Guar gum** | **0.6g** | 1/4 teaspoon. |
| **Fine salt** | **1g** | Enhancer. |

### Base protocol

1. **Base caramel:** in the gelato pan, first caramelise the 50g of sucrose. Once it's amber, add a little of the recipe's milk (carefully) to dissolve the caramel. You now have a caramel syrup.
2. **Lipids:** add the rest of the milk and the cream to the syrup.
3. **Solids dispersion:** mix the powders (remaining sugar, dextrose, milk powder, inulin, salt and guar) and add them to the liquid at **50 °C**.
4. **Homogenisation and pasteurisation:** run the immersion blender for 2 minutes and raise to **85 °C**.
5. **Maturation:** cool and leave in the fridge for **12 hours** so the caramel flavour settles.

---

## How to create the pockets (swirling)

1. **Churning:** churn the matured base in the machine (20-25 min).
2. **Assembly:** have the storage tub frozen. Lay down a layer of gelato, drizzle threads or thick drops of the Phase 1 sauce, another layer of gelato, and repeat.
3. **Final touch:** with a knife or spoon, make a single gentle circular ("S") motion. Don't over-mix or the pockets will merge into the base; we're after heterogeneity.

The base has a moderate PAC, but the swirl has a much higher PAC. That difference is what makes you find a liquid, salty caramel pocket while the gelato itself is firm.
