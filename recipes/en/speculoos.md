# Speculoos Gelato (800g)

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Whole milk** | **450g** | Continuous liquid base. |
| **Brown sugar (muscovado)** | **70g** | Provides the molasses and the dark colour. |
| **Sucrose** | **40g** | Only to melt and make the base caramel. |
| **Dextrose** | **85g** | Antifreeze (PAC). |
| **Skimmed milk powder** | **25g** | MSNF: proteins that hold the air. |
| **Inulin** | **15g** | Fibre to give body. |
| **Fine salt** | **1.5g** | A salty counterpoint that enhances the caramel. |
| **Guar gum** | **0.5g** | 1/4 teaspoon. |
| **Cream (35% fat)** | **130g** | The noble fat of the batch. |

### Spice mix

Mix them separately to have them ready:

- **Ground cinnamon:** 1.8g
- **Ground ginger:** 0.2g
- **Nutmeg:** 0.1g
- **Ground clove:** 0.05g (a knife tip; carefully)

---

## Protocol: Maillard reaction and infusion

### Step 1: Base caramel (pyrolysis)

1. Put the 40g of **white sucrose** in the pan over medium heat, without water.
2. Melt and watch it: we're after a dark brown (mahogany) caramel, smelling of toasted sugar, just before it burns. That's where the bitter compounds that recall Biscoff are.

### Step 2: Dairy shock

1. Carefully (it may splatter), pour the **milk** and the **cream** over the boiling caramel.
2. The caramel will harden from the thermal shock; that's normal. Keep stirring over low-medium heat and the heat of the dairy will dissolve it in a couple of minutes, tinting the milk a coffee colour.

### Step 3: Thermal dispersion (50 °C)

1. In a bowl, mix the remaining powders: brown sugar, dextrose, milk powder, inulin, salt, guar and **the spice mix**.
2. With the caramelised milk at about **50 °C**, add the powders in a steady stream.
3. Run the immersion blender for 30 seconds to disperse the guar and blend in the brown sugar.

### Step 4: Pasteurisation (85 °C)

1. Raise to **85 °C** for 1 minute to pasteurise and set the milk proteins.
2. Turn off the heat.

### Step 5: Long maturation

1. Cool quickly (bain-marie with ice) and leave in the fridge for a minimum of **12 to 24 hours**.
2. The essential oils of cinnamon, clove and ginger are fat-soluble: they need hours in the cold fat of the cream to release their aroma. The next day the flavour is much deeper.

### Step 6: Churning

1. Put the mix in the **freezer for 15 minutes** before churning.
2. Give it 15 seconds of immersion blender to liquefy the base.
3. Into the machine, **15-20 minutes**.
