# Piparra Sorbet (800g)

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Still water** | **435g** | Pure solvent. |
| **Pickled piparra peppers** | **120g** | Stems removed. Green, vegetal aroma. |
| **Piparra brine** | **20g** | A splash of the jar's vinegar; acidity and aroma. |
| **Extra-virgin olive oil** | **30g** | A micro-percentage of fat; improves the cut (Gilda effect). |
| **Sucrose** | **20g** | Almost none; only helps the texture. |
| **Dextrose** | **130g** | High dose of PAC: since it's all water and vinegar, it stops it becoming an ice cube, with low sweetness. |
| **Inulin** | **40g** | High dose; gives a sensation of body in the absence of dairy. |
| **Fine salt** | **3g** | Essential for the savoury profile. |
| **Guar gum** | **1g** | Half a teaspoon (water crystallises worse than milk). |

---

## Procedure (stabilised syrup)

### Step 1: Neutral syrup (hot)

1. Put the **435g of water** in the pan.
2. In a bowl, mix the powders (sucrose, dextrose, inulin, salt and guar).
3. At **50 °C**, add the powders in a steady stream, whisking.
4. Raise to **85 °C** for 1-2 minutes to dissolve the sugars and activate the inulin and guar. You're left with a transparent, slightly viscous syrup.
5. Turn off and let it drop to **40-50 °C** (so it doesn't burn).

### Step 2: Green emulsion (cold)

1. To the lukewarm syrup, add the **120g of piparra peppers**, the **20g of their brine** and the **30g of extra-virgin olive oil**.
2. Immersion blender at full power for **2-3 minutes**. The oil emulsifies with the water thanks to the viscosity of the inulin and the guar, and the piparra is blended completely. You're left with a translucent, silky green liquid.

### Step 3: Maturation and churning

1. Transfer to a tub and into the fridge for a minimum of **6-8 hours** so the guar traps the free water.
2. Into the machine. With no dairy fat, it will churn quickly, in about **15 minutes**.
