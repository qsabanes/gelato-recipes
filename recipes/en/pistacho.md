# Pistachio Gelato (800g)

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Whole milk** | **405g** | Main liquid base. |
| **Pure pistachio paste** | **100g** | The star: flavour, colour and unsaturated fats. |
| **Cream (35% fat)** | **90g** | Reduced; the pistachio already brings fat. |
| **Skimmed milk powder** | **30g** | MSNF: proteins to retain the air. |
| **Sucrose** | **65g** | Base sweetness. |
| **Dextrose** | **83g** | High PAC; keeps the gelato soft at -18 °C without being cloying. |
| **Inulin** | **25g** | Clean solids that improve viscosity and creaminess. |
| **Fine salt** | **1.5g** | Brings out the nut flavour. |
| **Guar gum** | **0.5g** | 1/4 teaspoon. Prevents a gummy texture. |

---

## Protocol (the emulsion)

The challenge with pistachio is blending the nut's oil with the water in the milk. A forced emulsion is needed.

### Step 1: Thermal dispersion

1. Pour the **whole milk** and the **cream** into the pan.
2. Over medium heat, once it reaches **50 °C**, add the dry-powder mix (sucrose, dextrose, milk powder, inulin, salt and guar).
3. Stir with a whisk so no lumps remain.

### Step 2: Pasteurisation and activation

1. Raise to **85 °C** and hold the heat for 1-2 minutes.
2. Here the whey proteins denature and are ready to trap the pistachio's fat.

### Step 3: Shearing

1. Turn off the heat and add the **100g of pistachio paste** to the hot pan.
2. Run the **immersion blender at full power for 1-2 minutes**. The heat and the revolutions break the oil into microscopic droplets that the milk proteins envelop, creating a stable emulsion. The colour shifts to a pastel green.

### Step 4: Maturation

1. Cool the pan quickly (sink with water and ice).
2. Transfer it to a sealed container and leave it in the fridge for a **minimum of 12 hours** (24-48 h if you do a double batch). The rest crystallises the pistachio fat and deepens the flavour.

### Step 5: Churning

1. Put the mix in the **freezer for 15 minutes** before churning (anti-grittiness trick).
2. Run the immersion blender for 15 seconds to break the guar gel.
3. Into the machine, **15-20 minutes**. With so much fine fat, it will churn quickly.
