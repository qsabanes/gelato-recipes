# Vanilla Gelato (800g)

Designed for supermarket vanilla pods (drier and less potent), which is why the infusion is prolonged.

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Whole milk** | **450g** | Continuous liquid base. |
| **Cream (35% fat)** | **165g** | The fat matrix that traps the aromas. |
| **Skimmed milk powder** | **35g** | MSNF: structural proteins. |
| **Sucrose** | **85g** | Clean sweetness that doesn't mask the vanilla. |
| **Dextrose** | **50g** | Antifreeze (PAC). |
| **Inulin** | **15g** | Silky body; prevents crystals. |
| **Fine salt** | **1g** | Brings out the sweet peaks of the vanilla. |
| **Guar gum** | **0.5g** | 1/4 teaspoon. |
| **Vanilla pods** | **3 units** | Aromatic reactor (more quantity because of their lower potency). |

---

## Protocol: extracting dry pods

### Step 1: Open up and multiply surface area

1. Split the **3 pods** lengthwise with a knife tip.
2. Scrape the inside to extract the caviar (the black seeds) and set it aside.
3. Cut the empty skins into pieces of about 2 cm with scissors. More cuts = more surface area for the milk to penetrate the dry wood.

### Step 2: Prolonged thermal infusion

1. Into the pan add the **milk**, the **cream**, the caviar and all the pieces of skin.
2. Over medium heat, at **50 °C**, add the premixed dry powders (sucrose, dextrose, milk powder, inulin, salt and guar), whisking.
3. Raise to **85 °C** (pasteurisation).
4. On reaching 85 °C, drop to the lowest setting (or turn off), cover the pan and **let it infuse for 5 minutes**. This is key to rehydrating the dry fibres of the pod so they release their essential oils.

### Step 3: Shearing and maturation

1. Run the immersion blender for **30 seconds** at medium power to distribute the caviar.
2. Cool quickly in the sink with water and ice.
3. Transfer everything to a sealed tub **leaving the pieces of skin inside**.
4. Mature in the fridge for **24 hours**. With supermarket pods, this long rest is mandatory.

### Step 4: Separation and churning

1. The next day, strain or fish out the pieces of skin and squeeze them well against the walls so as not to lose aromatised fat; discard them. The caviar passes through the strainer and stays in the gelato.
2. Put the tub in the **freezer for 15 minutes**.
3. Give it 10 seconds of immersion blender to liquefy the guar structure.
4. Into the machine with the paddles turning; in about **15 minutes** it will be ready.
