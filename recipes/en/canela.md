# Cinnamon Gelato (800g)

## Formulation

| Ingredient | Quantity (g) | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Whole milk** | **440g** | Base of the water phase. |
| **Cream (35% fat)** | **135g** | Dairy fat for a white base (~8% total fat). |
| **Skimmed milk powder** | **35g** | Provides the proteins that give structure. |
| **Sucrose** | **45g** | Reduced sweetness so the spice stands out. |
| **Dextrose** | **65g** | High PAC: softness through freezing-point depression, not gum. |
| **Inulin** | **15g** | Solids for body. |
| **Guar gum** | **0.5g** | 1/4 teaspoon. Stabilises without a gummy texture. |
| **Fine salt** | **1g** | Lifts the notes of the cinnamon bark. |
| **Cinnamon sticks** | **4 sticks** | Crushed. Dark Cassia or Ceylon variety. |
| **Ground cinnamon** | **1 tsp** | Added at the end, just before churning. |

---

## Extraction protocol (two-step kinetics)

The goal is to extract the **cinnamaldehyde** from the cinnamon while stopping the dairy notes from masking it, so the cold extraction is separated from the thermal phase.

### Step 1: Cold macro-extraction (24 h ahead)

- Crush the 4 cinnamon sticks (mortar or the base of a pan) into small pieces. More contact surface means more essential-oil transfer.
- Add the crushed cinnamon to the pan with the **whole milk** and **cream**.
- Cover and leave in the fridge for **24 h cold**. Cold lipid extraction captures the most volatile, floral aromas without "cooking" the milk.

### Step 2: Thermal phase and dispersion

- Put the same pan over low heat.
- At **50 °C**, add the dry-powder mix (sucrose, dextrose, milk powder, inulin, salt and the measured dose of guar).
- Blend briefly with the **immersion blender** just to combine, without breaking up the bark further.
- Raise to **85 °C** to pasteurise and activate the guar. Hold for 1 minute.

### Step 3: Straining and maturation

- Strain the hot syrup to remove the pieces of bark.
- Cool and return to the fridge for a **12 h maturation**. That's the time the milk proteins need to mature with the aroma already integrated.

### Step 4: Ground cinnamon (just before churning)

- Add **less than 0.5 g of good-quality ground cinnamon** to the syrup and stir.
- The suspended micro-particles give an aromatic opening hit and a subtle toasted colour that the dairy infusion alone doesn't provide.

### Step 5: Churning

- Churn in the machine for **20-25 minutes**.
